When to Consider Counselling

Any of these sound familiar? You don't need to be in crisis to reach out.

Anxiety, low mood, irritability, or shutdowns after school

School stress: focus, procrastination, test anxiety, attendance

Motivation, identity questions, or big life transitions

Family changes, grief (including pets), or social conflict

OCD and intrusive thoughts, overthinking, or worrying about future events.

Problem gaming, vaping/substance use, or online pressures

What to Expect

No forced sharing. No pressure. Every session is tailored to your teen, creating a safe space to explore challenges, build confidence, and develop practical tools for everyday life.

Start with a Conversation

We begin with a free 15-minute consultation to answer your questions, understand what’s been going on, and make sure counselling feels like the right fit.

A Collaborative Approach

Sessions are relaxed and conversational. Together we'll build practical strategies while helping your teen better understand their thoughts, emotions, and strengths.

A Safe, Confidential Space

Trust is essential. Teens are never pressured to talk before they're ready, and sessions provide a supportive, judgement-free environment where they can simply be themselves.

Flexible Session Options

Meet in my Brentwood Bay office, online anywhere in BC, or choose a walk-and-talk session if being outdoors feels more comfortable.

A calm, private space

A quiet counselling space in Brentwood Bay where teens and young adults can feel safe, comfortable, and understood from the moment they arrive. Bring headphones, a sketchbook, a fidget, or whatever helps you feel like yourself. This is your space.

For parents

Key info for parents

You know your teen best. My role is to support them while keeping you informed enough to feel confident in the counselling process—without breaking the trust that helps counselling work. Often when struggles show up in your teen you notice mood changes, anger, sadness or withdrawal. You may be concerned about their increased screentime, substance use, relationship or friend choices. Through counselling we will gently explore what is going on and have another person your teen can open up to without any judgement.

First step

What if they are hesitant?

This can be very common. Often I find after they have the chance to talk with me during a low pressure brief consultation they become open to trying it out. Many parents frame the consultation as a low stakes "let's just meet him and see what you think afterwards." You don't need him fully on board to reach out. Some parents start with the consultation on their own.

Teen Counselling FAQ

Real questions, honest answers

What happens when you reach out?

We start with a free 15-minute chat to see what you need and whether I'm a good fit. No commitment. If it feels right, we'll book a first session together and I'll send you a brief intake form.

What does a session feel like?

Relaxed and real. You set the pace. We can talk, take a short walk, or try practical tools. No forced sharing and no judgement.

Can I bring music, draw, or doodle?

Absolutely. If fidgeting, sketching, or music helps you think or feel calmer, let's use it.

Will my parents be told what I say?

Your privacy matters. I keep things confidential except when safety is a concern, such as risk of serious harm. I'll explain exactly how this works before we begin.

What if I don't know what to say?

That's completely normal. We can start with what's been difficult lately, or simply how today is going. I'll help guide the conversation naturally.

What if I don't like it?

You can tell me. We can adjust the approach, or if another counsellor would be a better fit, I'm happy to help you find one.

Can I just come for one session?

Absolutely. There is no minimum number of sessions. Some people come once, while others benefit from ongoing support.

What if I'm feeling pressure at school or on social media?

That's something we can absolutely work through together. We'll build practical strategies for stress, confidence, boundaries, friendships, and navigating social media in a healthier way.

Taking the first step is often the hardest.

You don't have to commit to counselling today. A free 15-minute consultation is simply a chance to ask questions, meet me, and decide whether it feels like the right fit—for both you and your teen.
