When to Consider Counselling

  • Anxiety, low mood, irritability, or shutdowns after school
  • School stress: focus, procrastination, test anxiety, attendance
  • Motivation, identity questions, or big life transitions
  • Family changes, grief (including pets), or social conflict
  • OCD and intrusive thoughts (ERP‑informed support)
  • Problem gaming, vaping/substance use, or online pressures

What to Expect

First sessions focus on building comfort, rapport and a plan. We set practical goals together, agree on what to try between sessions, and keep parents in the loop as appropriate. Confidentiality and boundaries are explained clearly.

How I Work with Teens

My main goal is building a relationship where my client feels safe and feels trust to open up. My approach is client‑centred and strengths‑based, using Solution Focused Brief Therapy, Narrative Therapy, and Cognitive Behaviour Therapy (CBT), including ERP when helpful. Many teens prefer active sessions — we can work outdoors or incorporate movement when it supports the work.

Key Info for Parents

  • Ages served: 10+ youth and young adults
  • Format: 50‑minute sessions, in person in Brentwood Bay or online
  • Caregiver involvement: collaborative, with teen consent and goals front‑and‑centre
  • Free 15‑minute consult to ensure fit
