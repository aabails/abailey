FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Questions I hear from teens and parents.

Starting counselling often comes with uncertainty. These answers cover some of the questions I hear most often.

What happens during the first session?

The first session is simply an opportunity to get to know one another. We'll talk about what's been happening, what's important to you and what you'd like counselling to help with. There's no pressure to share everything immediately.

What if my teen doesn't want counselling?

This is very common. Rather than forcing conversations, I focus on creating a comfortable environment where trust develops naturally. Many teens become much more engaged once they realize counselling isn't about being judged or told what to do.

Will you tell parents everything we talk about?

Counselling is confidential. Teens need to know they have a safe space to speak honestly. I'll always explain confidentiality clearly, including the situations where safety requires information to be shared.

How many sessions will we need?

Every person is different. Some concerns improve within a few sessions while others benefit from longer-term support. We'll regularly review progress together rather than committing to a fixed number of appointments.

Do parents attend sessions?

Depending on your teen's age and goals, parents may be involved in parts of the counselling process. We can discuss what approach is likely to be most helpful.

What ages do you work with?

I work with youth aged 10 and older, supporting them through the unique challenges that come with adolescence and early adulthood.

Do you offer online counselling?

Yes. I offer secure online counselling throughout British Columbia, along with in-person sessions in Brentwood Bay and walk-and-talk counselling when appropriate.

How do we get started?

The easiest place to start is by booking a free 15-minute consultation. It's an opportunity to ask questions, discuss your concerns and decide whether we're a good fit.

EARLY SUPPORT MATTERS

You don't have to wait until things reach a crisis.

Many families reach out because something simply feels different. Counselling isn't only for major mental health concerns—it can also help teens build resilience, develop healthy coping skills and navigate life's everyday challenges before they become overwhelming.

Early support can make a difference.

Learning healthy coping strategies early can help teens feel more confident managing future challenges.

Sometimes a neutral person helps.

Teens often find it easier to open up to someone outside their everyday relationships, creating space for honest conversations and fresh perspectives.

Asking for help is a sign of strength.

Reaching out takes courage. Counselling focuses on building strengths, confidence and practical tools rather than fixing what's "wrong."

When to Consider Counselling

  • Anxiety, low mood, irritability, or shutdowns after school
  • School stress: focus, procrastination, test anxiety, attendance
  • Motivation, identity questions, or big life transitions
  • Family changes, grief (including pets), or social conflict
  • OCD and intrusive thoughts (ERP-informed support)
  • Problem gaming, vaping/substance use, or online pressures

FAQ for Teens

What happens when you reach out?

We start with a free 15‑minute chat to see what you need and whether I’m a good fit. No commitment. If it feels right, we’ll book a first session together and I will send you the brief intake form.

What does a session feel like?

Relaxed and real. You set the pace. We can talk, take a short walk, or try practical tools. No forced sharing and no judgment.

Can I bring music/draw/doodle?

Absolutely. If fidgeting, sketching, or music helps you think or feel calmer, let’s use it.

Will my parents be told what I say?

Your privacy matters. I keep things confidential except when safety is a concern (for example, risk of serious harm). I’ll explain how this works at the start.

What if I don’t know what to say?

That’s really common. We can start with what’s been tough lately, or even just how today is going. I’ll ask gentle questions and we’ll figure it out together.

What if I don’t like it?

You can tell me - I can adjust the approach or I’ll help you find someone else who might be a better fit—no hard feelings.

Can I just come for one session?

For sure. There is no minimum, you can try a single session to see how it feels. Sometimes talking can feel a bit uncomfortable, so I recommend trying a few sessions.

What if I’m feeling pressure at school or on social media?

We can build a plan—boundaries, scripts for tricky situations, and quick skills for stress, sleep, and focus.
