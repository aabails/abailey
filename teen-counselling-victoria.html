TEEN QUESTIONS

Questions teens often ask.

It's completely normal to have questions before your first appointment.

Do I have to talk right away?

No. Some teens talk lots during the first session while others need time to get comfortable. There is no pressure to share anything before you're ready.

What if counselling feels awkward?

That's completely normal. Most people feel a little unsure at first. My goal is to create a relaxed environment where conversations happen naturally.

Will my parents know everything I say?

Counselling is confidential. We'll talk together about what confidentiality means and the rare situations where safety requires information to be shared.

What actually happens during a session?

Every session is different. We might talk through what's been happening, explore thoughts and emotions, develop new coping strategies or work toward goals that matter to you.

How many sessions will I need?

There isn't a set number. Some teens benefit from just a few sessions while others appreciate longer-term support. We'll regularly review how things are going together.

FOR PARENTS

Frequently asked questions.

How do I know if counselling is the right choice?

If your teen seems overwhelmed, withdrawn, anxious, struggling socially or simply isn't themselves, counselling can provide support before challenges become larger.

Can parents attend sessions?

Sometimes, yes. Depending on your teen's age, goals and preferences, parent involvement can be incorporated when it supports the counselling process.

What if my teen doesn't want to come?

This is common. Rather than forcing conversations, the focus is on building trust and creating an environment where your teen feels respected and understood.

How long is each appointment?

Standard counselling sessions are approximately 50 minutes, allowing enough time to explore concerns without feeling rushed.

Where are sessions offered?

Sessions are available in person in Brentwood Bay, online across British Columbia and, when appropriate, through walk-and-talk counselling.

FAQ for Teens

What happens when you reach out?

We start with a free 15‑minute chat to see what you need and whether I’m a good fit. No commitment. If it feels right, we’ll book a first session together and I will send you the brief intake form.

What does a session feel like?

Relaxed and real. You set the pace. We can talk, take a short walk, or try practical tools. No forced sharing and no judgment.

Can I bring music/draw/doodle?

Absolutely. If fidgeting, sketching, or music helps you think or feel calmer, let’s use it.

Will my parents be told what I say?

Your privacy matters. I keep things confidential except when safety is a concern (for example, risk of serious harm). I’ll explain how this works at the start.

What if I don’t know what to say?

That’s really common. We can start with what’s been tough lately, or even just how today is going. I’ll ask gentle questions and we’ll figure it out together.

What if I don’t like it?

You can tell me - I can adjust the approach or I’ll help you find someone else who might be a better fit—no hard feelings.

Can I just come for one session?

For sure. There is no minimum, you can try a single session to see how it feels. Sometimes talking can feel a bit uncomfortable, so I recommend trying a few sessions.

What if I’m feeling pressure at school or on social media?

We can build a plan—boundaries, scripts for tricky situations, and quick skills for stress, sleep, and focus.
