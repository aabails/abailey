Counselling on the trail.

Why walk-and-talk?

Nature helps

Research suggests that spending time outdoors calms the nervous system, reduces stress, and creates the conditions for meaningful conversations before we even begin talking.

Conversations come naturally

Walking side by side often feels less intimidating than sitting face-to-face. Many people find difficult conversations become easier when they're moving.

Less pressure

For many teens—and plenty of adults—there's less pressure to maintain eye contact or sit in a formal office. It simply feels more comfortable.

Building confidence

For anxiety and OCD, spending time outdoors can gently build confidence while practising new coping skills in everyday environments.

Reset & perspective

The steady rhythm of walking often helps thoughts settle, emotions regulate, and new perspectives emerge.

Whole-person care

Body, mind, and environment all influence wellbeing. Walk-and-talk counselling recognizes that healing doesn't only happen while sitting in a chair.

What a session looks like

Meet at the trailhead

We meet at a quiet, accessible trail near Brentwood Bay and walk at a comfortable pace—no hiking experience or fitness level required.

Honest, unhurried conversation

You'll receive the same thoughtful counselling as an office session—just with birdsong, fresh air, and cedar trees instead of four walls.

Confidentiality on the trail

We choose quieter routes and times whenever possible. If someone approaches, we'll simply pause the conversation until we've passed.

Weather-flexible

Coastal weather can change quickly. If conditions aren't suitable, we can move indoors, meet online, or reschedule for another day.

“Sometimes the fastest way through something is to walk beside it, not sit with it.”

Common Questions

A few questions people often ask before their first walk-and-talk session.

Where do we walk?

Quiet walking trails near Brentwood Bay—chosen for privacy, gentle terrain, and easy access from my office. I'll confirm the meeting location before your first outdoor session.

Do I need to be fit?

Not at all. We walk at a comfortable, conversational pace with plenty of opportunities to slow down or stop. If you have mobility concerns, we'll choose a route that works for you.

What if it's raining?

A little rain is usually fine (just bring a jacket!). If the weather is unsafe or uncomfortable, we'll simply move the session indoors, or reschedule.

Is it as confidential as an office session?

Yes. The same professional standards apply. We choose quieter routes and times, and if someone passes by, we'll simply pause our conversation until we have privacy again.

Can we mix walk-and-talk with office sessions?

Absolutely. Many clients do. Some weeks call for movement, while others feel better sitting down with a warm drink. We'll choose the setting that best fits you each session.
