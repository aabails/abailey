Counselling ● School Stress & Academic Pressure

School shouldn't feel like survival mode.

From test anxiety and procrastination to burnout and overwhelming expectations, school stress is one of the most common reasons teens and young adults reach out. Together we'll reduce the pressure, build practical skills, and help school feel like part of your life—not your whole life.

Parent, reading this for your teen? This page walks through what school stress often looks like, and how counselling can help — no need for your teen to be on board yet.

When school stress shows up

It isn't always about homework. Sometimes it's everything that comes with it.

Test & performance anxiety

Blanking on exams, spiralling before presentations, or feeling like every grade defines your future.

Procrastination

Knowing exactly what needs doing—but somehow never being able to start until the pressure is overwhelming.

Perfectionism

Anything less than perfect feels like failure. Rest feels unsafe, even when you're exhausted.

Attendance struggles

Stomach aches, panic before school, frequent absences, or simply not being able to walk through the door.

Peer pressure & social stress

Friendship drama, comparison, bullying, or the quiet feeling that everyone else has it figured out.

Academic burnout

Running on empty, losing motivation, feeling numb, yet pushing yourself harder every day.

How we'll work together

Less pressure. More confidence. Practical support that actually fits your life.

Understand the pressure

We'll figure out where the stress is really coming from— expectations, perfectionism, anxiety, workload, self-talk— so we know what actually needs to change.

Build real skills

Practical strategies for anxiety, procrastination, focus, boundaries, and motivation—tools designed to work in real life, not just in theory.

Redefine success

You are more than your grades. Together we'll create room for balance, confidence, and a healthier definition of what success looks like.

Support the whole system

When helpful—and with consent—I can collaborate with parents, caregivers, or school supports so everyone is pulling in the same direction.

Common Questions

Answers to a few questions students and parents often ask.

My teen refuses to see anyone. What do I do?

Start with a free 15-minute consult. We can talk through what's happening, ways to lower the barrier, and whether counselling is the right next step. Sometimes knowing what to expect makes all the difference.

Do you work with university and college students?

Yes. Many students from UVic, Camosun, and other post-secondary programs reach out for support with burnout, anxiety, perfectionism, identity, and balancing school with life.

Can we meet after school?

I offer after-school, evening and weekend appointments. Reach out and we'll find a time that works.

How is counselling different from tutoring?

Tutors help with schoolwork. Counselling focuses on the emotional side of learning—stress, anxiety, confidence, motivation, perfectionism, and burnout. Many students benefit from having both.

What if I'm just overwhelmed—not struggling academically?

That's exactly the kind of thing counselling can help with. You don't have to be failing school to deserve support. If school feels heavy, exhausting, or all-consuming, that's enough reason to reach out.
