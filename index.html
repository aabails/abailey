You Might Be Here Because

Something feels off — and you're paying attention.

Reaching out doesn't mean something is "wrong." It usually means someone you love is going through something hard, and you're looking for the right kind of support.

  • You ask how they're doing and get one word back before the door closes
  • They used to talk to you, and now they don't — about anything
  • They've pulled back from friends, activities, or things they used to care about
  • Mood swings, irritability, or anger that seem bigger than the situation
  • Grades slipping, sleep off, more time alone in their room or on a screen
  • You can tell something's wrong, but every conversation turns into a wall
WHAT SESSIONS ARE LIKE

Calm, collaborative, and moving at your pace.

No pressure to talk about everything at once. Together we look at what's really going on, build coping strategies that fit real life, and help things feel more manageable — one step at a time.

Step 1

A short call

A 15-minute phone or video chat — no pressure, no obligation.

Step 2

A real conversation

You share what's been going on. I listen and answer questions.

Step 3

A next step

If it feels like a good fit, we book a first session. If not, that's okay too.
