THE FIRST SESSION

A collaborative start — not a checklist.

Our first session is about getting to know you. We'll explore what's bringing you in, what's been feeling difficult, and what you'd like life to look like moving forward. There's no pressure to tell your whole story—we'll move at a pace that feels comfortable.

Together we'll decide what feels most helpful and develop a plan that fits your goals, strengths, and everyday life.

MY APPROACH

Safe, comfortable, and client-centred.

I believe counselling works best when you feel heard, respected, and genuinely understood. My role isn't to judge or tell you what to do—it's to help you make sense of what's happening, discover practical strategies, and build on the strengths you already have.

Registered Clinical Counsellor BC Association of Clinical Counsellors · RCC #19338
THERAPEUTIC APPROACH

Evidence-based approaches, tailored to you.

I draw from several evidence-based approaches, blending them to fit your unique situation rather than following a single method. Therapy is always guided by your goals, strengths, and what will be most helpful in the moment.

Person-Centred

Creating a safe, accepting space where your experiences and strengths guide the conversation.

Solution-Focused Brief Therapy

Building on what's already working while identifying practical next steps toward meaningful change.

Narrative Therapy

Exploring the stories you tell about yourself and creating room for new perspectives.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

Learning practical tools to better understand thoughts, emotions, and behaviours.

Exposure & Response Prevention (ERP)

An evidence-based approach that helps reduce anxiety and OCD through gradual, supported exposure.

Family Systems

Understanding how relationships, communication, and family patterns influence wellbeing and growth.

TEEN COUNSELLING

Steady support during a big developmental stage.

Adolescence brings constant change—at school, at home, in friendships, and in how young people see themselves. These years can be exciting, overwhelming, and everything in between.

I work with youth ages 10 and older, helping them navigate anxiety, ADHD, OCD, school stress, identity, grief, family changes, and the many pressures that come with growing up. My goal is to create a space where they feel understood while building practical tools they can use beyond the counselling room.

THE SPACE

A calm space, set apart.

My counselling office is located in a private garden studio on my Brentwood Bay property, thoughtfully designed to feel calm, welcoming, and separate from a traditional office. From the moment you arrive, the goal is simple: to create a space where teens feel comfortable enough to be themselves.

Warm natural light, comfortable seating, and peaceful surroundings help create an environment that feels relaxed rather than clinical—making it easier for conversations to happen naturally.
