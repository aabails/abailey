Getting Started with Journaling in Sessions

Published: September 14, 2025

Journaling can be a powerful CBT tool with teens—if it’s framed as a quick, low‑friction way to externalize thoughts and track patterns, not as “homework.” Here’s a simple structure you can use right away.

The 3×3 Prompt

  • 3 facts: what happened?
  • 3 feelings: name them (0–10)
  • 3 forward steps: tiny actions before next session

Make It Stick

  • Use phone notes or a small card; keep it under 3 minutes.
  • Anchor to a routine (bus ride home, after practice, before bed).
  • Review the last entry together at the start of each session.

That’s it—short, repeatable, and easy to personalize.
